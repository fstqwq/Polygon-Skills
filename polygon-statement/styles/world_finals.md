# World Finals Style

Use this style by default.

Reference examples: `<skills>/polygon-statement/styles/icpc2025_excerpts.md`

## Core approach

- Start by identifying the concrete scene.
- Decide who the solver is in that scene and what role they play.
- Map each abstract object, operation, and goal to something concrete in the story.
- Write the mathematical content through the story rather than as a separate abstraction block.

## Rules

- Use a story or scenario. The scene and the problem should be woven together, not split into a "story paragraph" followed by a "math paragraph".
- If the user gives an abstract problem, turn it into a concrete story unless the user explicitly asks for a more direct style.
- Keep the story realistic enough to carry the definitions cleanly.
- Use second person "you" when it helps the reader inhabit the scenario naturally.
- Humor is natural, not forced. If a funny aside fits the flow, include it. Do not bolt on jokes or add filler words.
- Math definitions are embedded in prose. Introduce variables and constraints as part of the narrative.
- Input/Output sections are format-only.

## What good output feels like

- The reader can picture the situation before seeing variable names.
- The story directly defines the objects and constraints.
- The transition from setup to task feels continuous, not like a theme pasted onto an abstract statement.
